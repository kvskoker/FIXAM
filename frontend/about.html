FIXAM

Facilitating Issue eXchange for Accountable Municipalities
Event: Big 5 Game Changers Hackathon (MoCTI, Sierra Leone)
Theme: Public Service Architecture Revamp
Repository: github.com/kvskoker/FIXAM
Team: Kenneth V.S. Koker (Lead Developer) & Ibrahim Jalloh (Researcher)

1. Executive Summary

FIXAM is a comprehensive civic engagement ecosystem that empowers citizens to report municipal issues via WhatsApp and enables authorities to manage, analyze, and resolve them efficiently through advanced AI and automation.

By utilizing a "Hybrid Bridge" architecture, the system combines the accessibility of conversational interfaces (WhatsApp) with the analytical power of geospatial technology (GIS/Maps), bridging the gap between informal community grievances and formal government infrastructure planning.

2. The Solution: "Chat-to-Map" Ecosystem

FIXAM introduces a seamless workflow where the input is conversational (low barrier to entry) but the output is structured operational intelligence.

Step A: ü§ñ Intelligent WhatsApp Bot

The primary reporting channel, designed for maximum accessibility.

  • Conversational Reporting: Guided flow for citizens to report issues naturally.
  • Voice-to-Text: Native support for Voice Notes (Krio/English), transcribed locally via Whisper.
  • Evidence: Users can send photos or videos as proof.
  • Safety First: Automated content moderation filters unsafe images (e.g., nudity) using localized AI.
  • Location Intelligence: Accepts GPS pins or text addresses with reverse geocoding.
  • Automated Feedback Loop: Citizens receive real-time status updates via WhatsApp when their issue is acknowledged or resolved.

3. Technical Stack

The project prioritizes open-source technologies to ensure sustainability.

Frontend: Vanilla JS, Leaflet JS Backend: Node.js, Express, Socket.io Database: PostgreSQL + PostGIS AI: Whisper, NudeNet, Qwen3-0.6B (Local LLM) Integration: Meta WhatsApp Business API

4. Impact Potential

  1. Democratizing Infrastructure Data: Giving every citizen with a phone the power to act as a city inspector.
  2. Enabling Data-Driven Governance: Moving from reactive, anecdote-based planning to proactive, heatmap-based resource allocation.
  3. Increasing Trust: The transparent status tracking (Red to Green pins) rebuilds trust between the government and the public.
  4. Citizen Rewards (Gamification): Earning digital points for reporting and verifying issues incentivizes consistent participation and civic pride.
